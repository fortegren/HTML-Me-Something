About Me

My name is Francesca. I'm originally from Texas--born in San Antonio, then lived in Austin and DFW for a few years. When I graduated high school, I moved to Lubbock, TX, where I lived until I got married in 2014.

My husband, Marco, and I just built an amazing forever-home with our two dogs, Mason [photo] and Auggie [photo]

Education, Career, and Future

In Lubbock, I attended Texas Tech University, where I earned a BA, MA, and PhD in Experimental Psychology. While I was working on my dissertation, I got married here in St. Louis [photo of location], moved to Carbondale, IL for a year [while my husband worked at SIU], then took a job in Evansville, IN at Southern Indiana University for three years. This year, I moved to the St. Louis area. I currently teach psychology at McKendree University.

My trainig is in experimental psychology with a focus on cognition. Particularly how people regulate their own study behaviors to learn new information, how people think about their own learning, and how educators can help students become more efficient learners.

My career, however, has been more focused on teaching than on research (which wasn't my plan, just the way it worked out with the two-academic household). While I enjoy aspects of what I do, my real interests lie in programming (the "logic" and problem-solving really speak to me) and data science. I think data science--particularly machine learning and artificial intelligence--are changing the way we interact with the world. The area of data science involves also my nerdy love of statistics and likely some "teaching" or communicating numbers to people, which I love (I just don't love teaching them how to do it themselves haha).
